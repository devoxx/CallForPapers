Presentation Guidelines

Hardware

All speakers can use their own machine, using any operating system. Signal output should be 60 Hz.

The audio/video crew will provide a VGA, HDMI and Thunderbolt connector for your portable.

Don't do a presentation on batteries as this will result in a 'weak' video signal.

Screen Resolution

The ideal (16:9) resolution for your portable is 1280 x 720.

Internet

We provide a wired internet connection in each room for the speaker, never rely on shared conference wifi for a demo! Devoxx champions have a backup plan just in case internet access isn't working, for example making a screen recording video of your online demo (if possible).

Presentation Template

Devoxx Belgium has an optional presentation template>.

If you use your own then please add your twitter handle in the footer and also mention the "#Devoxx" hashtag and another unique tag which defines your talk. This will help you identifying related tweets to your talk.

Make sure you also have a copy of your slide deck somewhere somewhere on the cloud, you never know!

Speaker Preparation Room

During conference days (Wednesday and Thursday) one of the BOF rooms near the reception serves as a speaker preparation room.

Testing Equipment

Every Devoxx cinema room has an A/V specialist who can help you with equipment questions or can arrange some hardware testing during longer breaks.

Recordings

Your presentation will be recorded (except if it's a Hands On labs, Ignite or BOF session). These will be made available on our YouTube channel the next day.

Pictures on Flickr

The majority of the speakers are being photographed by our team and these are made available on our Flickr channel, royalty free.

Q&A

Make sure you repeat the questions if the second mic is not used during Q&A. If not, the Q&A will not be included in the 'online' recorded version.

Presentation Timing

We use digital clocks to count the number of minutes that each speaker has available in his/her talk.

Please note that this year the conference talks are 50 minutes.

FAQ

If you still have questions then have a quick look at our FAQ page.
